Shift 1 · Render path (v4.2: pipeline corrected)

v4 said: founder renders motion via Higgsfield/Seedance 2.0 from ChatGPT-generated start + end keyframes. v4.2 says: Seedance 2.0 is text-to-video, not image-to-video — founder confirmed two-keyframe input failed in B3 v2 iteration. ChatGPT is OUT of the production pipeline entirely. New three-way lane: architect writes the Seedance text prompt; founder iterates renders in Seedance until tonality lands; CD specs the visual target and fits the MP4. Two-render pattern (landscape 16:9 + portrait 9:16) carries unchanged from v4.1 — the multi-aspect rationale doesn’t depend on the input shape. CD lane on motion: spec + fit, still never produce.

Shift 2 · Driver (forced by shift 1)

Seedance produces a forward-time MP4 between two keyframes. v3 specced the driver as scroll-scrubbed (CSS animation-timeline: view() playing rendered frames at scroll progress). CSS scroll-driven animation cannot drive HTMLVideoElement.currentTime, and scrubbing a flat MP4 frame-accurately is a JS pattern (Rule #2 violation). The v3 driver is incompatible with the v4 asset.

v4 resolution: driver becomes scroll-triggered, not scroll-scrubbed. The bridge zone fires the spread once on viewport entry (small IntersectionObserver calling video.play() at threshold 0.6 — not an animation library, not a scroll listener, so Rule #2 holds). After settle, end-state ambient field holds via CSS gradients / SVG. Reverse-on-scroll-up becomes “held” once seen, not retract. Spread fires every page-visit, no storage hook — different motion class from B3's rip. Architect call (v4): scroll-scrubbed flirts with “responsive interactive web app” energy — productivity-tool reframing leak risk (Rule #4). Trigger-once = product asserts its own pacing; visitor witnesses. See §04.

Shift 3 · B4.PULSE split out

v3 absorbed the at-rest mark pulse (the B3.B4.PULSE reservation) into B4 as §06. v4 splits it: B4 ships spread only. B4.PULSE becomes its own deliverable after B4 closes — different motion class (subtle perpetual rhythm vs one-shot spread), different timing logic, different production path (PULSE is pure CSS keyframe on the static B3 final frame; no Seedance render needed). Splitting matches the pause-and-report discipline in CLAUDESIGN.md v4. Sequence: B4 (spread) → pause → B4.PULSE → pause → B5.

Shift 4 · Starting-state geometry lock (v4.2)

The B3 settled mark exists in two reference versions: v1 (wide horizontal mass spanning the frame width, dense throughout) and v2 (vertical pour with widening base, denser at the bottom, narrower above). v4.2 locks the geometry against v2. The v3 draft's spread language — “pour, downward, narrow at origin, widening toward dispersal” — composes against v2's vertical-with-widening-base, not v1's horizontal mass. v4.1 absorbed that shape language without naming the v1/v2 distinction; v4.2 names it explicitly so the geometry lock is on the record. The spread's start state is the v2 settled mark; the start state geometry is locked. If founder produces a v3 B3 asset later, the spread keyframe-equivalent (the visual-target start state in §07) needs a v4.3 reconciliation.

What survives unchanged

  • Shape of the spread (§03) — pour, downward, narrow at origin, blooms at tip, disperses into ambient field. Liquid grammar holds.
  • Destination = ambient field, not individual tendrils (§05). The discovery-vs-delivery argument is unaffected by the driver change.
  • Mark persists, does not deplete (§05). Fallback chain (§06) — in fact the fallback-on-no-scroll-driven branch becomes the primary pre-baseline path under v4.
  • No fade primitive. Every transition continues via translate / scale / box-shadow / chroma.

03

Shape of the spread

Downward, narrow at origin, widening toward dispersal. Not radial-from-mark (would re-centre attention on the mark when attention should be moving past it). Not horizontal-sweep (wrong axis — scroll is vertical). Not particle-burst (too retail-FX). Slime pours — gravity-aware, liquid, formless, with a leading tip that softens as it widens.

The four moves of the spread shape

  • Break. The resolved mark loses its perimeter integrity at the bottom edge. Edge softens, then begins to thread downward. Slime is no longer a body; it is becoming a flow.
  • Pour. A narrow vertical stream descends along the bridge axis B1 reserved. Width ~14–22% of the mark; not a sheet, not a drip. The bridge hairline becomes the seam it tracks.
  • Bloom. Past the bridge midpoint, the leading tip widens. The flow itself stays narrow; the tip is where the spread happens — a soft circular bloom expanding ahead of the descent.
  • Disperse. By the time the bloom reaches the constellation region, it has lost its locus and become an ambient field — a low-chroma slime-tint that suffuses the space around the hotspots. The hotspots are inside the field; the field is what wakes them.

Rejected shapes (and why)

  • Radial-from-mark — re-centres attention on the mark. The whole point of the spread is to release the visitor downward into the constellation. The mark must become source, not destination.
  • Horizontal sweep — wrong axis. The scroll is vertical; horizontal motion fights the scroll direction.
  • Particle burst / spark trail — reads as retail product FX or game-engine sparkle. Wrong register; slime is wet and formless, not granular.
  • Multiple streams — too organised. Two streams reads as a fork; three reads as a logo. Single stream stays formless.
  • Mark fades into ambient field — Rule #9. Mark stays at its B3 settled state, anchored at the top of the bridge; the spread is a new layer descending from it, not the mark dissolving.

What “wet” means here

The spread reads as liquid behaviour, not particles, not gas. Soft-edged at every scale. Edges blur, never crisp. The flow's silhouette deforms continuously as it descends — no held shapes, no rigid contours. Where it pools at the tip, surface tension reads as swell. Where it disperses around the hotspots, it reads as ambient saturation, not vapor. The aesthetic ancestor (DC-12) is alien biological liquid, not retail neon.

04

Driver: scroll-triggered, once

B3 was time-driven on page entry. B4 is scroll-triggered — the spread fires once when the bridge zone enters the viewport as the visitor moves past the hero. After settle, the end-state holds; no retract on scroll-up. Spread fires every page-visit, not once-per-session. (v4 revision: v3 specced scroll-scrubbed; that conflicts with the v4 founder-renders-MP4 asset shape. See §00.)

Why scroll-triggered, not scrubbed

Scrubbing would tie every frame of the spread to scroll position — cinematic, but it forces the asset shape to be a CSS-driven scene (transforms / gradients / masks), not a rendered video. v4 canon is the opposite: founder-rendered video outperforms CSS hand-animation for violent organic motion. Trigger-once preserves the rendered-asset advantage and still lets the spread feel like the page's response to the visitor scrolling past the hero — the spread doesn't fire until the visitor moves.

Deeper read (architect v4): scroll-scrubbed motion has “responsive interactive web app” energy — the visitor steering the brand mascot frame-by-frame with their trackpad. That flirts with productivity-tool reframing (Rule #4 leak risk). Koy's identity is the product asserting its own pacing; the visitor witnesses, doesn't steer. Fire-once at bridge-zone-enters-viewport is the right grammar.

It also avoids a subtle UX failure mode: scroll-scrubbed motion feels fidgety on trackpad scroll (small reversals cause micro-shudders in the asset). A one-shot trigger plays clean.

Why “held,” not “retract”

v3 proposed reversibility — scroll back up, spread retracts, mark recomposes. That was natural under scroll-scrubbing. Under trigger-once it would mean firing the asset in reverse on scroll-up, which Seedance output doesn't guarantee — organic liquid motion is not bidirectionally symmetric. v4 picks “held.” Once the visitor has seen the spread, the ambient field stays. Scrolling back up reveals the hero with the mark intact and the field already established below. This resolves the v3 open item B4.PERSIST in favor of persistence.

Gating · fires every page-visit (no storage hook)

B3's rip has koy.landing.rip.seen — first-impression violence, protected from over-tax by once-per-session gating. B4 spread is a different motion class: transitional ambient, not first-impression. It earns its re-fire on every visit; refreshing the page re-plays it. No storage hook. The page-visit IS the gate — the IntersectionObserver fires the first time the bridge zone enters viewport on that load and disconnects. Subsequent scrolls past the bridge during the same load do nothing.

Architect v4 call. Documented here so future instances don't accumulate spread under a session-storage hook by reflex.

Implementation · the two timelines

  • Trigger = tiny IntersectionObserver on the bridge zone, threshold: 0.6. Fires video.play() once on entry; observer.disconnect() immediately after. NOT a scroll listener, NOT an animation library — Rule #2 holds (the rule bans CSS-in-JS frameworks and JS animation libraries, not the platform observer API). Dev A integration: ~8 lines, no dependency.
  • Internal motion = rendered into the asset itself. Phase easing (pour = snap-like, mid-spread = glide-like, disperse = pulse-like) is baked into the keyframes founder feeds to Seedance and the interpolation it produces. --ease-rip is NOT reused — that curve belongs to landing-scale arrivals only (B0 reservation).
  • Settle = video element's native ended event. One inline onended handler sets data-state="settled" on the bridge zone. CSS attribute selector reveals the ambient-field overlay (gradient + soft halos, position: absolute over the same zone). Video stays as last-frame poster underneath; field overlays on top. No JS animation, no setTimeout chain.
  • Hotspot wake = pure CSS choreography cascading from [data-state="settled"] on the bridge zone, via per-hotspot CSS animations with staggered animation-delay (§05). No JS.
05

Destination: the ambient field

Does the slime reach the hotspots? Yes — as an ambient field, not as individual touches. The spread's leading tip widens into a soft saturation that fills the constellation region; the hotspots sit inside the field, not at the end of trails. The field is what wakes them.

Why ambient field, not individual trails

Six tendrils reaching for six hotspots is too organised — reads as a mind-map or skill-tree. Worse, it makes the hotspot positions feel predetermined by the slime; the visitor reads the spread as “the slime is delivering me to these six specific things,” which is brand-positioning, not arrival. The ambient field is the opposite: the slime arrives, the constellation is already there inside it, the visitor's eye finds the hotspots because the field illuminates them. The hotspots feel discovered, not delivered.

Hotspot wake state · frame, never fill

Architect v4 call: hotspot wake is slime-tinted at the edge, chrome-neutral at the fill. The wake acts as a frame around the template thumbnail, never as a tint over it. Phase C templates (Bloomberg-dense, Glam Girl Y2K, others TBD) each ship their own aesthetic identity; the chrome's wake must announce “alive” without imposing alien-techno-green onto a hot-pink Glam Girl thumbnail. Museum-wall logic: white walls, any paintings.

  • Dormant ring · 1px solid oklch(0.40 0.05 148 / 0.30) at the hotspot tile boundary. Present but low-key — not invisible (Rule #9 has no opacity 0).
  • Woken ring · 1px solid oklch(0.65 0.12 148 / 0.70) at the same boundary. Phosphor outline, low chroma, narrow band. Transition is a chroma + lightness shift on the existing border, NOT an opacity ramp.
  • Outer wake halo · box-shadow: 0 0 12px oklch(0.85 0.09 148 / 0.30) on woken, 0 0 0 oklch(0.85 0.09 148 / 0) on dormant. Driven by the box-shadow spread + alpha animating together (alpha goes 0 → 0.3, not 0 → 1; the shadow's presence is what fades up via radius growth, not via opacity). Read as soft alive-glow at the frame, not a fill.
  • Slime tendril · the spread's connection point at each hotspot. Bright slime, oklch(0.85 0.09 148 / 0.85) (clamped-reality hue, matching B3 sampled values — see §08). Width 8% of tile width at hotspot attach, tapering to ~2% toward the spread origin. Does NOT cross the tile boundary into the content area. Enters via translate-from-spread-origin, NEVER opacity ramp.
  • Tile interior · untouched. No overlay, no tint, no shadow on the content. The template's own aesthetic full-bleed inside the ring. This is the rule that lets Phase C templates breathe.

Wake choreography · CSS cascade from [data-state="settled"]

The wake cascade fires together at the asset's ended event — not at scroll position. Under trigger-once, the field arrives all at once at settle; sequential awakening is preserved as a baked-in CSS choreography rather than scroll-driven staggering.

  • Trigger · CSS attribute selector .kl-bridge[data-state="settled"] .kl-hotspot activates the woken-state animation on each hotspot.
  • Stagger · animation-delay per hotspot, derived from each hotspot's distance from the spread origin (top-center). Hotspot 1 (closest, top-left at 14% / 68%) wakes first; hotspot 5 (farthest, bottom-center at 58% / 92%) wakes last. Stagger interval ~140ms. Total cascade window ~700ms.
  • Curve · --ease-pulse on each hotspot's wake animation. Asymmetric organic — reads biological, not mechanical.
  • Duration · --dur-base = 480ms per hotspot for the ring chroma shift + halo bloom. Tendril translate-in arrives ~80ms after the ring activates (the field reaches the boundary, then the connection threads in).

B4's wake spec hands off cleanly to B5

B4 owns the wake transition (dormant → woken) and the resting woken state (ring + halo at static intensity). B5 will own the interactive woken state (hover, focus, click, navigation, template-gallery bridge). The frame-vs-fill rule locks now and B5 inherits it: B5's hover and click states must continue to leave the tile interior untouched. Tendril behavior on hover (does it brighten? pull back?) is B5's call, not B4's.

What happens to the mark during the spread?

The mark stays at its B3 settled state, anchored at the top of the bridge zone. It does not deform, it does not deplete (the spread does not drain the mark; slime is not finite). At scroll 100% the mark is still at the top of the hero, visible if the visitor scrolls back up. The mark is the spring; the spread is the water; the spring does not empty.

06

Fallback chain

Four failure modes. All resolve to the same end-state: constellation visible, hotspots woken, mark held above. No spread motion, no wake cascade — just the resolved scene. The fallback path is a quieter landing, not a broken one.

Trigger Detection Behavior
prefers-reduced-motion CSS media query (Phase A canon). Spread asset NOT loaded. Bridge zone immediately renders data-state="settled". Ambient-field overlay shown directly. Hotspots in woken state from page load — no wake cascade. Mark held at B3 settled state.
no-autoplay (mobile Safari quirk) video.play() returns rejected promise. .catch() handler in the IntersectionObserver fires the same settled-path: data-state="settled" attribute set, ambient-field + woken hotspots revealed without playing the asset. The visitor on a strict autoplay-blocked browser sees the resolved scene at bridge-zone-entry, not nothing.
asset-load-fail Video onerror. Same as no-autoplay path. Bridge zone goes to data-state="settled"; field + wake cascade still fires.
low-bandwidth 600ms timeout race (same contract as B3). Same as asset-load-fail. Static field-still PNG (§07) substitutes for the video frame.

The fallback principle

The spread is the gift; its absence costs nothing. Constellation works without it. Hotspots wake without it (different trigger, same end state). Mark is the same in either case. The visitor on a fallback path sees a quieter landing, not a broken one.

Production pattern · three-way lane

Step 1 — CD (this doc, §08): writes the visual target. Start state, end state, tonality, what to avoid. CD-voice, NOT a Seedance prompt.

Step 2 — Architect: converts CD's visual target into a Seedance 2.0 text prompt. Likely two prompts — one per aspect ratio. Architect shows CD the prompts before founder runs them, so CD can flag tonality drift.

Step 3 — Founder: runs Seedance with each prompt. Iterates the prompt with architect until tonality lands. Exports MP4. ~5 min per generation; iteration count unknown — depends on how cleanly Seedance reads “biological wet liquid” vs slipping into particle FX or stylized organic.

Step 4 — CD (later deliverable): integrates the two MP4s into landing chrome. Fits, doesn't alter.

Two correctly-composed motions preserve the spread's silhouette at each aspect. This is the right pattern for any future per-aspect motion — locks at B4.

Tonality (architect: this is the load-bearing read)

  • Biological wet liquid. Reads as a living organism that happens to be liquid — not as paint, not as water, not as goo. Surface looks moist. Edges deform softly as the body moves.
  • Slow, decisive, alive. Internal pacing: sharp break at start (the pour begins decisively), calm widening through middle (the descent breathes), organic gentle settle at end (the field saturates without splashing). Internal easing is the asset's job — Seedance bakes it.
  • Silent. No audio. The asset is mute even at the source.
  • Mark is the spring, not the drain. The mark above stays full while slime descends. Slime is not finite. The viewer should never read the mark as emptying.
  • The void substrate is present from start to end. The substrate is the canvas the slime saturates; it never goes black-on-black, never disappears.

What to avoid (architect: these are the failure modes)

  • No particle FX. No spray, no dust, no glitter, no specks. The slime is a continuous body of liquid; it does not atomize.
  • No splash, no splatter. When the column meets the field, the field saturates outward smoothly — no impact, no droplets, no ring-shock.
  • No rigid contours. Edges stay soft throughout. The slime never reads as a CGI 3D-modeled object with hard silhouette.
  • No mark deformation. The mark above the seam stays motionless. If Seedance starts to drift the mark, it's an iteration target.
  • No camera motion. Frame is static. No pan, no zoom, no rotation. The slime moves; the camera doesn't.
  • No additional characters or imagery. Just slime + void + seam. No hotspots in the rendered asset — the constellation arrives via CSS overlay, not via the render (this prevents the hotspot positions from being baked into the asset and going stale if B1 grid shifts later).
  • No fade. Asset has no opacity ramps anywhere. (Rule #9 applies even inside the rendered asset — the field arrives via chroma saturation against void, not via opacity 0 → 1.)
  • No granular texture. The slime reads as continuous liquid body, not as voxel/pixel/grain effect.

Aspect-specific notes

  • Landscape — the field is the bottom horizontal band. Spread reach is lateral. Hotspots will overlay across the field's width.
  • Portrait — the field is the central vertical column at the bottom. Spread reach is depth, not lateral. Hotspots will overlay stacked down the column.
  • Both — same start mark (the v2 settled mark), same tonality, same hue stack, same duration target (~2.4s), same internal pacing. The two renders differ only in where the slime goes after it leaves the seam.
