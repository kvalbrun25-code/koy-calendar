What the eye should see in the new timing

A held void (the 100ms pre-flash registers as compression — a moment of held breath before the rupture) → a sharp bright flash → a jagged vertical seam opens and stays open long enough to read as a wound (this is the change — v1 closed the seam before the eye locked it) → an amorphous slime-green mass blooms from inside the seam, pushing the tear edges open → the tear retracts to a faint perimeter glow as the mark settles. No bounce. Overshoot via --ease-rip means the mark crosses its final position before snapping back — a tiny pull, not a wobble.

Why the founder's instinct was right

380ms is the chrome-snap budget — the duration a toast pops in. Calibrated for acknowledgement, not emergence. Rip-from-void is the project's motion soul (DC-11); it carries DC-10's alien menace, DC-12's slime arrival, the Constitution's whole “from year 3026” thesis. Compressing it into a chrome-snap window flattens it into a brand sting. 840ms is still one event to the eye (under the 1-second threshold where viewers start wondering “is this loading?”) but it is long enough for the seam to seam. The load-bearing phase is the tear; everything else paces around it.

02

Shape of the rip

Vertical, jagged, center-anchored. Not radial (too superhero-burst), not horizontal (too curtain-open), not diagonal (too slash-the-screen). Vertical tear is the rip's natural axis — inherited from DC-10 / DC-11 visual language (something forcing its way OUT of the void) without lifting recognizable composition.

Rejected shapes (and why)

  • Radial burst — reads as Marvel superhero arrival. Too iconic-in-the-wrong-way; identical to a dozen brand stings.
  • Horizontal slit — reads as a curtain or shutter opening. Domestic, not violent.
  • Diagonal slash — reads as a knife or claw mark. Too violent in the wrong register (aggression-toward-viewer rather than emergence).
  • Multi-slash / cross-tear — too busy; even with 840ms the eye can't lock to two seams; the rip wants a single point of rupture.
  • Mark fades in — Rule #9. Not a candidate, never was.

What "jagged" means here

The seam is NOT a clean line. It zigzags ~4–8% horizontally as it descends, like a torn cloth seam or a lightning fork. The jaggedness is what reads as ORGANIC tearing rather than mechanical aperture. In production, the renderer chooses noise frequency, edge thickness, and seam-glow falloff — keep it irregular, keep the edges hot.

What "violent" means here

Force, not aggression. The void doesn't break gently; it gets pried apart by something pushing through. The pre-flash compression is the moment of pressure; the tear is the rupture; the bloom is the mark forcing into space. No screen-shake. No glitch artifacts. Violence is in the motion's CONFIDENCE, not its noise.

Does the void recover?

Yes. By t=840ms the tear has retracted to a faint hairline glow around the mark's perimeter. The void is whole again, but it's marked — the rip leaves a residue, not a wound. On subsequent visits (once-per-session triggered), the page opens in the recovered state. The rip is an event; the static final-frame is the steady state.

03

Timing

840ms total for the rip itself. B2's hero-composition window slides accordingly — phrase enters at the new t=840ms, cue at t=1160ms. Easing curve = --ease-rip (the cubic-bezier B0 designated for landing-scale only, never spent in chrome). One curve does the whole rip; the four phases vary only in WHICH ELEMENT moves at which point on the curve. Tear-open is the load-bearing phase — its budget grew the most relative to v1.

Why 840ms (and not 380 or 1200)

v1 specced 380ms; founder watched the runner and the tear was over before the eye registered the violence — the green flash arrived before the seam could read as a seam. That is a flicker, not an emergence. At the other end, 1200ms slips past the “single event” ceiling — by ~1s the visitor starts thinking loading screen, and the rip loses authority. 840ms sits inside the architect's 700–900ms target band, leaves the tear-open phase ~400ms (room for the seam to read), and still resolves as one event. Comparable to a hard scene cut + breath in film grammar.

Phase budget reasoning

  • Pre-flash (100ms) — enough to register as compression / held breath; not enough to feel like waiting. Sits at the snap-toast threshold.
  • Tear opens (400ms, 47.6%) — the load-bearing budget. The eye must see the void splitting. Seam holds near peak for ~100ms within this window so it lands as a held wound, not a passing crack.
  • Mark emerges (200ms) — the slime bloom. Long enough to read as force (something pushing through), short enough to feel inevitable.
  • Settle (140ms) — overshoot resolves. Tear retracts to perimeter glow. This is where --ease-rip's 1.35 overshoot fires.

What happens at t=840ms+

B2's spec inherits with the window shifted. Phrase enters at t=840ms (translate-only, snap easing, ~80ms after mark settles). Scroll cue hairline at t=1160ms. Skip affordance was present from t=0 and remains present until the page transitions out of the hero scroll-range. B2's spatial staging unchanged — only the timing window slides.

04

The mark · unified

The thing that rips into being. Formless on purpose. Unification confirmed by the architect: the rip-mark is slime, in its settled form. One mascot, two modes — rip (arrival in B3) and spread (takeover in B4). The editor chrome's typographic koy mark (Phase A) is the third, threshold-crossed state. Same identity, three surfaces.

The single-thread arrival narrative

Slime tears into existence (B3) → resolves into the mark (B3 settle phase) → spreads downward (B4) into the space between hero and hotspots. One continuous gesture. A visitor sees the same green presence acting in three modes and recognizes ONE thing as the platform's avatar; everything else is just the page. Constitution's “Bloomberg-meets-MySpace” balance gets reinforced — the strange creature carries the alien-techno-trippy authorship, the user canvas stays unsupervised.

Why “formless” survives a momentary resolved state

Formlessness is slime's identity property, not a hard constraint on every frame. The mascot is motion-formless and spread-formless; at the moment it lands as the landing-page brand mark, it permits a momentary resolved state — then becomes formless again as it spreads in B4. Three states: motion-formless → landing-resolved → spread-formless. IP discipline (DC-10/11) holds because nothing about the resolved state is a recognizable silhouette — it is still an irregular soft-edge blob, just at rest.

How this surfaces across the product

  • Landing hero (B3) — slime mid-arrival; motion-formless → landing-resolved over 840ms.
  • Bridge → hotspots (B4) — slime spreads from the settled rip-mark, downward, into the constellation. Same entity continuing.
  • Editor chrome (Phase A) — the typographic koy mark in the toolbar is the threshold-crossed state. Slime has settled, the system is dense and Bloomberg-trustworthy. The same identity, expressed as type instead of as a presence.
  • Nav brand-slot on landing (B1) — reserved data-asset="rip-mark" slot receives the static final-frame asset (post-rip resolved state).

What the resolved mark looks like

  • Amorphous — irregular soft-edge blob; border-radius: 48% 52% 56% 44% / 50% 46% 54% 50% baseline, varied per render.
  • Slime-green — radial gradient from oklch(0.92 0.290 148) core to oklch(0.55 0.210 148) edge, anchored on the canon 148° hue.
  • Soft-edged — 2px blur at minimum. NO sharp vector outline. NO eyes. NO body. NO recognizable silhouette. Slime is presence, not character.
  • Settles to ~60vmin — the hero rip-slot B1 reserved.
  • Lives lightly — at rest, a sub-perceptible breathing pulse (post-rip, separate from the 840ms event). Pulse rhythm itself deferred to B4.PULSE; B3 sets only the rest shape.
05

Skippable affordance

Discreet glyph in the bottom-right of the hero. Present from t=0 — visible BEFORE the rip starts, not surfacing during. (Surfacing during would mean fading in. Rule #9.) Once dismissed or once-completed, the storage flag fires; subsequent visits skip the rip entirely and open in the final-frame state.

Skip intro esc

Behavior

  • Always present from t=0. Not animated in. Static styled element from initial paint.
  • Click / tap / Esc all jump to final-frame state immediately (mark settled, phrase placed, cue rendered).
  • Once the rip completes naturally OR once the user skips, set the storage flag. Subsequent visits skip the rip entirely.
  • Skip stays visible through the hero scroll-range. As the visitor scrolls past the hero, Skip leaves with the hero — it's a hero-scoped affordance, not a global one.

Storage contract (Dev A wires backend)

Single boolean key. Suggested name: koy.landing.rip.seen. Read at hero mount: if true, open in final-frame state (no rip motion, no skip glyph). If false, play the rip + show skip glyph. Set on rip-completion OR on user-skip OR if reduced-motion / asset-load-fail. Dev A picks the storage backend (sessionStorage vs localStorage vs server-side per-account) based on whatever the auth lane decides. Spec only the contract.

CSS class to ship (B3 → koy-landing.css)

The skip affordance is a new chrome element on landing. Suggested class: .kl-skip. Hairline pill, mono caption, kbd glyph inline. The styling is shown in the demo above; the actual CSS sits in B3's PR to koy-landing.css. Dev A appends; Phase A canon is not touched.

06

Fallback chain

Three failure modes; all resolve to the same fallback — the static final-frame asset, rendered at t=840ms. No transition, no fade, no decoration. The rip is the gift; its absence costs nothing.

Trigger Detection Behavior
prefers-reduced-motion CSS media query, zeroed at root (A1 canon). Open in final-frame state immediately. No rip. No skip glyph (nothing to skip). Storage flag set so subsequent visits behave consistently.
low-bandwidth Spec only the contract: if the motion asset hasn't loaded within 600ms of hero mount, fall back. Detection via simple timeout race. (Network Information API is not universal; Dev A may use it as a hint but timeout is the floor.) Open in final-frame state. Storage flag NOT set — visitor may retry later under better conditions.
asset-load-fail Video / image onerror. Open in final-frame state. Storage flag NOT set.
storage:seen koy.landing.rip.seen === true at mount. Open in final-frame state immediately. No rip (already seen). No skip glyph (nothing to skip).

The final-frame is itself an asset

Per architect: two assets. Production renders the motion artifact AND a static final-frame artifact. The static is used in every fallback path AND in the B1 nav .kl-nav__brand-slot (the small post-rip mark in the top-left chrome). Same source render, different output formats.
