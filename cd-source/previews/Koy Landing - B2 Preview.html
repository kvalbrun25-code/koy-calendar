01

Voice direction

What B2 extends from the A1 stand-in ("make it yours.") and what it explicitly refuses. The constraints below are the working sieve every candidate passes through.

  • ClaimReads like the user, not the brand.No "Koy" in the phrase. No platform-positioning ("a place where," "the canvas for").
  • ConcretePost-rip context makes the referent specific. "this" / "yours" / "mine" all resolve against the page that just ripped into existence — they are not abstract claims.The rip-mark gives every phrase its referent. The phrase does not need to over-explain.
  • PersonalFirst- or second-person, with second-person framed as the user speaking themselves (not the brand speaking to them).The strongest voice is the user inhabiting the words.
  • DefiantTerse. No qualification. Sentence case + period — declaration, not tagline.Period is small but load-bearing. It's the difference between announcing and selling.
  • Verb disciplineNo effort verbs (build / create / design / make as labor-framed action)."make it yours." works because the verb serves ownership, not labor. Defiant voice often skips verbs or uses existential imperatives.
  • Rip-targetShort enough to land in the first second after the void tears open. No subordinate clauses. The strongest version stands alone."make it yours." length (14 chars) is the ceiling. Two-line subhead allowed only if it strengthens; propose without first.

What B2 explicitly refuses

  • Build your page
  • The world has a page
  • Your rules
  • The Ultimate ___ Experience
  • A place where ___
  • Productivity-tool framing
  • Booking-software framing
  • NSFW framing or innuendo
  • Brand-omniscient voice
  • Coach / motivational pulls ("be the ___")

04

Spatial staging

The rip-mark and the phrase share the void. The call: vertical stack — rip-mark above, phrase below, scroll cue at the foot. Reading order is mark → phrase → cue, top-down, eye falling naturally with the rip's gravity. B3 inherits this composition; the rip animation builds INTO this spec, not around it.

B3 · mark

this is mine.

Scroll

Why phrase BELOW mark

The rip-mark is the project's motion soul (DC-11). At hero scale (60vmin square) it is the dominant gravitational element on the screen. Placing the phrase BELOW lets the eye fall naturally — rip explodes, eye locks on the mark, phrase resolves as the consequence.

Phrase ABOVE the mark forces the eye UP after the rip — unnatural reading order, weakens the rip's gravity.

Mark-as-bullet beside phrase shrinks the mark to a glyph, underselling B3.

Mark BEHIND phrase (overlapping) is the boldest option but the hardest to render at hero scale and risks the mark losing individuality.

Time sequence (spec for B3)

The composition resolves over ~0.9s after first paint. Phrase entry is translate-only — Rule #9 holds. Snap easing, ~80ms after mark settles.
